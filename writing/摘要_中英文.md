# 摘要

针对高超声速飞行器再入与临近空间飞行过程中等离子体鞘套引发的“黑障”问题，电子密度动态诊断是认识电磁传播机理和支撑抗黑障设计的重要基础。传统微波干涉法以相位为观测量，在高电子密度条件下面临整周模糊；宽带线性调频连续波（LFMCW）时延法虽可将微小时延映射为差频频率偏移，但在强色散条件下，群时延的频率依赖性会导致差频信号出现频谱散焦、峰值偏移和线性反演失效。为解决这一问题，本文不再将色散视为待抑制的干扰，而是将差频信号中的群时延演化轨迹作为新的观测量，在阐明色散等离子体中LFMCW传播机理的基础上，研究时延特征提取、降维参数反演及其工程验证。

本文基于Drude模型推导了非磁化等离子体的复介电常数、传播常数与群时延解析表达式，建立了全频段频率-群时延非线性映射模型，定义了群时延非线性度因子，并给出了基于局部线性化近似的工程判据$\chi(f_c)=B\cdot \eta(f_c)\cdot \tau_0 \lesssim 1$，用于表征传统LFMCW时延诊断方法的局部适用边界。该方法通过对全频段差频信号进行FFT分析，提取其主峰对应的差频频移，进而换算传播时延，并在远离截止频率的近似条件下将时延映射为电子密度。理论分析表明，碰撞频率$\nu_e$对群时延的贡献为$(\nu_e/\omega)^2$量级的二阶微扰，而电子密度$n_e$决定群时延曲线的主导形态。据此，本文采用固定$\nu_e$、仅反演$n_e$的降维策略，并构建了滑动窗口—MDL信源数估计—TLS-ESPRIT特征提取—Metropolis-Hastings MCMC反演的处理链路。该方法通过逐窗重构频率-时延特征轨迹并引入幅度加权似然函数，实现了电子密度反演及不确定性量化。

在理论与仿真验证方面，本文构建了由MATLAB解析计算和CST全波电磁仿真组成的双重验证框架。结果表明，截止频率附近群时延呈显著渐近发散，电子密度主导时延曲线形态，碰撞频率主要影响幅度衰减；单点观测信息不足，参数辨识存在明显不适定性；引入宽带频率—群时延曲线后，可显著增强参数可辨识性并改善反演稳定性。Drude模型仿真表明，在强色散条件下，传统整段差频信号FFT主峰检测方法的电子密度反演误差可超过100%，而本文方法在SNR为20 dB时的反演误差优于0.5%，在10 dB至30 dB范围内保持稳定；后验分布分析表明，$n_e$为强可观测参数，$\nu_e$为弱可观测参数。

在工程与等效验证方面，本文完成了Ka波段宽带LFMCW诊断系统设计，将系统扫频带宽由800 MHz提升至3 GHz。移动靶标标定结果显示，系统在3 GHz配置下实现了3.33 ps的最小时延分辨率，按诊断信号中心频率32 GHz、等离子体直径200 mm计算，对应电子密度诊断下限约为$1.24 \times 10^{17}$ m$^{-3}$。此外，本文采用微波带通滤波器作为色散等效验证载体，而非最终诊断对象，分别在ADS全链路仿真环境与实测硬件系统中完成了去嵌入、时延轨迹提取和参数一致性检查。ADS仿真基线与实测滤波器的双重验证结果表明，所提方法能够从LFMCW差频信号中稳定提取色散介质的群时延轨迹特征；当色散函数的数学形式已知时，这些特征点可进一步驱动后续参数反演。

研究结果表明，本文方法拓展了传统"单峰检测—单值时延"方法在强色散条件下的适用边界，实现了基于群时延轨迹特征的参数反演。相关结果为高电子密度等离子体的动态诊断提供了新的技术途径，并为真实等离子体环境中的在线测量与抗黑障应用提供了可迁移的方法链路。

**关键词：** LFMCW；等离子体诊断；色散介质群时延轨迹；时频分析；参数反演

# Abstract

Accurate dynamic measurement of electron density is essential for understanding electromagnetic propagation in plasma sheaths and for blackout mitigation in hypersonic flight and atmospheric reentry. Conventional microwave interferometry uses phase as the observation quantity and therefore suffers from phase ambiguity in high-density plasma. Although linear frequency-modulated continuous-wave (LFMCW) delay measurement avoids phase wrapping by converting propagation delay into beat-frequency shifts, it still fails under strong dispersion, where the frequency dependence of group delay causes beat-spectrum defocusing, peak bias, and breakdown of linear inversion. Instead of treating dispersion as interference to be suppressed, this thesis uses the group-delay trajectory embedded in the beat signal as a new observable and studies LFMCW propagation in dispersive plasma, group-delay trajectory extraction, reduced-order parameter inversion, and engineering validation.

Based on the Drude model, the complex permittivity, propagation constant, and group delay of unmagnetized plasma are derived, and a full-band nonlinear mapping between probing frequency and group delay is established. A group-delay nonlinearity factor is introduced, An engineering criterion based on the local linearization approximation, $\chi(f_c)=B\cdot \eta(f_c)\cdot \tau_0 \lesssim 1$, is further established to characterize the local applicability boundary of the conventional LFMCW full-band FFT peak-shift-based diagnostic method. In this method, the beat signal is processed by a full-band FFT, and the propagation delay is estimated from the frequency shift corresponding to the spectral main peak, which is then mapped to the electron density. Theoretical analysis shows that the effect of collision frequency $\nu_e$ on group delay is only a second-order perturbation of order $(\nu_e/\omega)^2$, whereas electron density $n_e$ governs the dominant shape of the delay curve. Accordingly, a reduced-dimensional strategy is adopted by fixing $\nu_e$ and inverting only $n_e$. On this basis, a processing chain of sliding-window analysis, MDL-based source number estimation, TLS-ESPRIT feature extraction, and Metropolis-Hastings MCMC inversion is constructed to reconstruct the frequency-delay trajectory and quantify inversion uncertainty through an amplitude-weighted likelihood function.

For theoretical and simulation-based validation, a dual framework combining MATLAB analytical calculation and CST full-wave simulation is established. The results show that group delay exhibits pronounced asymptotic divergence near the cutoff frequency, that electron density dominates the delay-curve shape, and that collision frequency mainly affects amplitude attenuation. They also show that single-point observation provides insufficient information and leads to an ill-posed identification problem, whereas introducing the wideband frequency-group-delay curve significantly improves identifiability and inversion stability. Simulations based on the Drude plasma model indicate that, under strong dispersion, the conventional inversion method based on a single FFT peak over the entire beat signal can produce errors exceeding 100%, while the proposed method keeps the inversion error below 0.5% at an SNR of 20 dB and remains stable from 10 dB to 30 dB. Posterior analysis further confirms that $n_e$ is strongly observable, whereas $\nu_e$ is only weakly observable.

For engineering validation using an equivalent medium, a Ka-band wideband LFMCW diagnostic system is developed, and the sweep bandwidth is increased from 800 MHz to 3 GHz. Moving-target calibration demonstrates a minimum delay resolution of 3.33 ps, corresponding to a lower detectable electron density of approximately $1.24 \times 10^{17}$ m$^{-3}$ for a 32 GHz probing center frequency and a 200 mm plasma diameter. A microwave bandpass filter is further employed not as the final diagnostic target, but as a controllable equivalent medium with a known dispersion model. Both ADS full-link simulation and hardware measurements with the physical filter are used for de-embedding, trajectory extraction, and parameter consistency verification. The dual validation confirms that the proposed method can stably extract group-delay trajectory features from the LFMCW beat signal, and that these features can further drive subsequent parameter inversion once the mathematical form of the dispersion function is known.

The results demonstrate that the proposed method extends the applicability of conventional plasma diagnostics beyond the single-peak, single-delay paradigm under strong dispersion and enables parameter inversion based on group-delay trajectory features. These results provide a new approach for dynamic diagnosis of high-density plasma and support future online measurement and blackout-mitigation applications in real plasma environments.

**Key words:** LFMCW; plasma diagnostics; group-delay trajectory in dispersive media; time-frequency analysis; parameter inversion
